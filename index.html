About

Clinician. Engineer. Builder.

I am an emergency physician and Director of ED Data Analytics and Machine Learning at Mayo Clinic, where I lead a cross-functional team building GCP and BigQuery cloud infrastructure and AI systems for one of the busiest and most complex academic emergency departments in the country. Formally trained in clinical informatics since 2019, I have been building AI systems in healthcare since 2022.

My work sits at the intersection of clinical practice and engineering. I see patients in the ED, then analyze the data from those same encounters to build models that make the department safer and more efficient. I have built 11 AI systems end-to-end — from data extraction and feature engineering through model development, validation, and deployment — 5 of which are currently in active clinical use.

I am also the technical founder of emergencymedicine.app, a HIPAA-compliant RAG platform built on GCP with Gemini 2.0 Flash, awarded a $200,000 Mayo Clinic Presidential Strategic Initiative Fund (PSIF) grant and selected for Mayo Clinic Platform Deploy. I chair Mayo Clinic's enterprise Location Services Solutions Group — a 25+ member cross-functional committee overseeing the largest healthcare RTLS installation in the US — and serve as Physician Chair of the enterprise RTLS RFP ($18.5M).

I am board certified in Emergency Medicine (ABEM) and Clinical Informatics (ABPM), hold an MBA from Tuck at Dartmouth, an MD from Geisel at Dartmouth, and an MS in Health Informatics from Northwestern. I have 21 peer-reviewed publications and have delivered 40+ national and international presentations.

AI Systems

11 Systems Built. 5 in Active Clinical Use.

Live in Deployment

Emergency Medicine App

HIPAA-compliant RAG platform on GCP. 168 EM protocols. Gemini 2.0 Flash streaming answers with inline citations. Awarded $200K PSIF grant. Selected for Mayo Clinic Platform Deploy.

Live in Deployment

ED Surge Forecasting

XGBoost + DNN predicting census states 4, 8, and 12 hours ahead. Trained on 165,285 encounters. AUC up to 0.91. Live in GCP; integrated with telemedicine dispatch. Best Abstract, Mayo Clinic AI Summit 2025. Published MCP Digital Health, Dec 2025.

Live in Deployment

Admission Prediction

Gradient boosting model with AUC 0.84–0.94 across sites with per-site calibration. Deployed directly in Epic EHR.

Live in Deployment

Waiting Room Deterioration Detection

Two-stage ML + rules-based model flagging ESI-3 patients at risk of sepsis, ICU escalation, or resuscitation before rooming. Live in the emergency medicine viewer.

Live in Deployment

Short LOS / Fast Track Prediction

Gradient boosting identifying patients likely to disposition within 60 min while still in the waiting room. Live in GCP for pod-based rooming and terminal shift optimization.

GCP Deployment Setup

GNN ED Bottleneck Detector

Graph Neural Network modeling the ED as a patient-staff-asset graph using live RTLS data. Detects bottlenecks before gridlock. Validated at two sites. Manuscript in revision.

Validated

ED Billing Level Prediction

NLP + ML ensemble predicting E&M CPT codes from clinical notes. 321,893 encounters. AUC 0.94–0.95. Published Ann Emerg Med 2025. Cited 9 times.

Validated

UTI and Antibiotic Sensitivity Prediction

XGBoost models for urine culture positivity and antibiotic sensitivity. AUC 0.70–0.93 across 100,000+ encounters. Published Heliyon 2025 and Sci Rep 2025.

Validated

Geriatric Bounceback Clustering

Unsupervised ML identifying 19 distinct return-visit phenotypes among high-risk older adults. Most Valuable Presentation, WACEM 2024.

Validated

Lab Draw Forecasting

MultiOutputRegressor with 6 parallel time horizons (+1 to +6 hours). Validated on 80/20 time-ordered split of retrospective BigQuery data.

Validated

NLP Workup Prediction

TF-IDF + tabular ensemble predicting likely orders from triage notes for proactive workup planning. Presented at national conference.
